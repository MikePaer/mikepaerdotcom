About Me

I’m a musician, software engineer, and dog person.

Music

I’m a classically trained pianist and composed my first album Piano Action! back in 2008, inspired by talents like Dustin O'Halloran and Ludovico Einaudi. Then life happened, and while I kept playing piano privately (and started fiddling around with synthesizers and loop pedals), I took a break from creating new music for the public.

Fast forward a decade to 2018 — I got a second wind and ventured into a new musical direction: electronic/ambient music with piano roots. After learning how to use my favorite new toy, Ableton Live – a digital audio workstation, I released my second full-length album, Momentary Masters, on March 20, 2018. Inspirations include Little People, Emancipator, Kiasmos, Saltillo, and Niklas Paschburg.

Technology

I became interested in technology & computers at a very young age, and that passion has stuck with me my whole life. I got a degree in computer engineering and joined the workforce as a technical product manager at a big tech company. When I’m not making music, I’m likely working on a side project – an app, tool, or website – to simplify my life, automate my home, or to just stay up-to-date on the latest software development trends.
